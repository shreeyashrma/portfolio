About

Scientist. Communicator.
Industry-Ready.

From vaccine manufacturing floors in Nepal to genomic research labs at Clemson — a rare blend of GMP rigor, molecular expertise, and global perspective.

Shreeya Sharma is a Ph.D. candidate in Animal and Veterinary Sciences at Clemson University whose research sits at the intersection of microbiology, immunology, and microbiome science. She has authored more than a dozen peer-reviewed publications in leading journals such as Nature Scientific Reports and PLoS ONE, and is a two-time recipient of the Clemson University Interdisciplinary Research Fellowship (2024 & 2025).

Recognized for science communication, Shreeya earned second place in the Clemson Three Minute Thesis competition and second place in the Clemson Data Visualization Contest (2026). Her research integrates molecular biology, immunology, and microbiome sequencing to address one of the most pressing challenges of our time: reducing antibiotic dependence while protecting both animal and human health.

Before her doctoral work she served as a Research Associate at BIOVAC Nepal — a GMP-aligned vaccine manufacturer — where she led a U.S. Department of State Biosecurity Project and built the regulatory fluency that sets her apart. She is a fellow of Clemson's Accelerate to Industry (A2i) program, an active peer reviewer for well-established scientific journals, and an industry collaborator with AGRITX Biotech.

Beyond the Lab

Raised in a military family, Shreeya brings a rare discipline and composure to everything she does — thriving under pressure, meeting deadlines without compromise, and leading with quiet confidence. Outside the lab, she keeps life full: playing pickleball with her fiancé, experimenting in the kitchen, swimming laps to reset, and riding horses whenever the opportunity arises. She believes the best science comes from people who live fully.
